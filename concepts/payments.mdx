Kellogg Ticket Hub processes all payments through [Stripe](https://stripe.com), a PCI-compliant payment platform trusted by thousands of businesses worldwide. Your card data never touches Kellogg Ticket Hub's servers — it travels directly from your browser to Stripe, which returns a secure token that the platform uses to confirm and record your purchase. This architecture keeps your financial information safe by design.

## Payment Flow

When you check out for an event, the following steps happen in sequence:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Initiate Checkout">
    You select your tickets and proceed to checkout. Kellogg Ticket Hub creates a Stripe Checkout Session and reserves your seats for up to 15 minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stripe Presents the Payment Form">
    You are redirected to (or shown) the Stripe-hosted payment form. This page is served directly by Stripe, not by Kellogg Ticket Hub.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You Enter Payment Details">
    You type your card number, expiry, CVC, and billing address — or select a saved payment method or digital wallet. These details go straight to Stripe and are never forwarded to Kellogg Ticket Hub.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stripe Confirms Payment">
    Stripe authorises the charge with your card network and notifies Kellogg Ticket Hub via a secure webhook that the payment has succeeded.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ticket Hub Creates and Emails Your Tickets">
    Kellogg Ticket Hub receives the confirmation, generates your confirmed ticket records, and sends QR codes to your registered email address.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Supported Payment Methods

## Payment Statuses

Every payment moves through a series of statuses that reflect where it is in the processing pipeline. The table below explains each one.

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `requires_payment_method` | The checkout session is open and waiting for you to enter or select a payment method. |
| `requires_confirmation` | Payment details have been collected and the charge is ready to be confirmed. |
| `processing` | Stripe is communicating with your card network to authorise the charge. |
| `succeeded` | The payment has been authorised and captured. Your tickets are confirmed. |
| `canceled` | The checkout session expired or was abandoned before payment was completed. Your seat reservation has been released. |

<Note>
  If a payment moves to `canceled`, no charge has been made to your card. You can start a new checkout session at any time while the event still has available capacity.
</Note>

## Currency

All transactions on Kellogg Ticket Hub are processed in **US Dollars (USD)**. Ticket prices displayed on the platform are always in USD. If your card is issued in a different currency, your bank applies its own exchange rate and may charge a foreign transaction fee — check with your card issuer for details.

## Receipts and Confirmations

After a successful payment you receive two separate emails:

1. **Stripe receipt** — sent by Stripe to your billing email, this confirms the charge amount, last four card digits, and a transaction reference.
2. **Ticket confirmation** — sent by Kellogg Ticket Hub to your account email, this contains your event details and QR code(s) for entry.

<Tip>
  If your payment fails, double-check your card number, expiry date, CVC, and billing postcode before retrying. Mismatched billing information is the most common cause of declined payments. If the problem persists, try a different card or contact your bank to confirm the transaction is not being blocked.
</Tip>
